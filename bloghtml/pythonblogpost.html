A First Time Coder

Right here in the city of Kampala, my imaginative soul, often lost in the world of work and pursuit for a meaning to life, on a sunny afternoon, as the sky painted hues of pink and orange, I stumbled upon an advert for Certificate in Software Engineering at Refactory Academy.

Intrigued by the idea of creating something on the vast canvas of the internet, I decided to give coding a try by applying for this interesting course. Even before the start of the course, boom!, I loaded with pre-work to learn html, css and; git and github. Armed with a notebook, a laptop, and a cup of coffee, I settled into a cozy corner of my room where I normally sit when I want to concentrate. Progate and the YouTube tutorials, hosted by several friendly coders, promised to guide beginners through the enchanting world of web development gave me an encouragement to press on.

The tutorial continued with an introduction to CSS, the magical language that could style and beautify the HTML elements. I learned how to change colors, fonts, and layouts with a few simple commands. It was like painting on a digital canvas, and my imagination ran wild with possibilities.

With each passing minute, my new exciting webpage took shape. A header here, a paragraph there, and soon enough, the webpage transformed from a plain text document into a visually appealing creation. I couldn't help but feel a sense of accomplishment and pride as she previewed her work in the browser.

The real magic happened when I stumbled and played with CSS. Adjusting margins, experimenting with different fonts, and adding a splash of color brought the webpage to life. It was at this point when I realized that coding was not just a technical skill but a form of creative expression, a way to craft a unique digital space.

With the portion of the Tutorials I managed to watch on YouTube, I marveled at the webpage I had created literally from scratch for the verys first time. It was a simple site, but it represented a journey into the unknown, a leap into the world of coding. I definitely felt a newfound sense of empowerment and curiosity. The once-intimidating code now seemed like a language I could speak all the time and also enjoy speaking, a language that opened doors to endless possibilities.

From that day forward, my curious interest in coding blossomed. HTML and CSS were just the beginning of a thrilling adventure into the realms of Python, responsive design, and dynamic web development. As the stars appeared in the night sky, I closed my laptop with a smile, knowing that I had just embarked on a journey that would shape my longed for creative path for years to come.

Don mess up with my codes, I am coming for you Python, Django and all of you relevant programming languages
